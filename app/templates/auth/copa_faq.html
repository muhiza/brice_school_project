The answers you seek.

What kind of cooperatives would use COPA platform?

COPA solution was developed for all cooperatives from all economic sectors, but currently the software is fully developed for agricultural cooperatives in it's early days, while other sectors can also use the solution and improve their management style, the satisfaction is not guaranteed.

Can unions, federations and confederations use COPA as well?

Yes, the inspiration, design and implementation of COPA platform is based on Cooperatives' umbrella.

How members of the Cooperatives benefits from the platform?

As cooperatives are democratic owned and controlled, we take that into consideration in everything we do. Members of cooperatives can easily track the performance of their cooperative, on daily basis and participate in everything going on within the cooperative.

Where does Blockchain meet COPA?

Blockchain technology allows to record, share and synchronize transactions in their respective electronic ledgers (instead of keeping data centralized as in a traditional ledger). A blockchain powered e-wallet will drastically improve trade finance, syndicated loans, and tracking provenance of products and commodities, their subsequent sale or use as financing collateral, improving the overall efficiency of Rwandan cooperatives.

  • Distributed ledgers:​ Putting all financial transactions on a blockchain allows to digitise the loan administration and disbursement process of several financial institutions, from SACCOs to traditional banks, while at the same time improving governance of cooperatives through transparency of membership contributions, cooperative finances and much more.
  • Digital tokens:​ Using blockchain technology such as ethereum’s ERC20 standard to create digital tokens representing real world assets, to prove ownership of any physical asset, from precious metals to cars to real estate, or equities and bonds. This will greatly improve efficiency of different value chains, increase liquidity of products and services, and open up different sectors for financing and investment.
  • Smart contracts:​ Delay in payments is one of the biggest problems in the Rwandan economy. Using smart contracts and innovative trade financing enables corporates to delay invoice payments while ensuring that small suppliers have access to capital while they wait by setting up payment when goods are delivered or services are performed. Smart contracts, combined with third party information or IoT can also revolutionise insurance, so that in case of a flood for example, a smart contract is executed automatically for payout for the concerned area.
  • Traceability:​ Supply chain traceability: in many supply chains, a blockchain can assist in providing an immutable record from the provenance to the retail store of a product. This can give consumers increased trust in the products that they buy and it is also an opportunity to reward the producers who employ good practices. This will eventually lead to sustainable production practices and responsible consumption. This ensures food safety and also helps in reducing food frauds and increasing brand reputation.

We work with different institutions from government, private and non benefits organisations to provides capacity building services to all users of the platform.
